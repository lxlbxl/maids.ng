01 — Logo Identity

The Mark We Carry

The Maids.ng logo is a bubbly, rounded wordmark — warm, playful, and distinctly approachable. It is the single most important brand asset and must be treated with precision.

Logo Character Analysis
🔵

Rounded, bubbly wordmark

Thick custom letterforms with extreme rounding signal approachability, warmth, and friendliness — not corporate, not luxury. The logo sets the emotional register for the entire brand.

✨

Sparkle / star details inside letterforms

The sparkle motifs embedded in the "a", "i", and "d" letter counters introduce magic and delight — they signal cleanliness, freshness, and a hint of the premium without formality. This is a key brand equity element.

⚪

White stroke outline

The thick white border around the letterforms gives the logo strong legibility on any background and contributes to its sticker-like, tactile quality. Always preserve this stroke in applications.

🎨

True brand colour: #0F5556 (Deep Teal)

Extracted directly from the logo file via pixel sampling. Not the assumed #1A5C45 forest green, and not a cool navy. This dark teal anchors the entire colour system.

📝

.ng in smaller, lighter weight

The TLD sits visually subordinate but is essential — it signals Nigerian identity and domain ownership simultaneously. Never separate it from "maids".

Logo Variations
Clear Space & Minimum Sizes

Clear space: Minimum 25% of logo width on all sides. No text, images, or graphic elements within this zone.

Minimum digital size: 120px wide. Below this the sparkle details and letterform curves degrade.

Minimum print size: 30mm wide at 300dpi. Use vector source file for print.

Light backgrounds only at natural colours. Use white-inverted version on teal, dark espresso, or photographic overlays.

The sparkle motif is a brand equity element — never remove or obscure it. It is visible from 120px width and above.

13 — Do's & Don'ts

The Rules We Live By

Non-negotiable. These protect everything the brand stands for.

✅Always use #0F5556 as the primary brand teal — derived from the actual logo
❌Never substitute a different green, navy, or teal that doesn't match the logo
✅Use Cormorant Garamond for all hero headlines and emotional display moments
❌Never mix typefaces outside the defined trio (Cormorant / DM Sans / DM Mono)
✅Show real, diverse Nigerian faces — helpers AND families authentically
❌Never use stock photography that could be set anywhere in the world
✅Treat helpers as professionals — confident, skilled, proud in all imagery
❌Never depict helpers in cap-and-apron uniforms, subservient poses, or stereotyped roles
✅Preserve the sparkle/star motif in all logo applications — it's brand equity
❌Never distort, recolour, rotate, or recreate the logo
✅Use green-tinted shadows (rgba(15,85,86,…)) — never grey box-shadows
❌Never use gradient backgrounds — flat design only throughout the UI
✅Write ₦10,000 (naira symbol, no space) — always for Nigerian prices
❌Never write NGN or add a space between the symbol and number
✅Follow the 8pt grid for all spacing decisions on every layout
❌Never use random spacing values outside the defined scale
✅Keep WhatsApp messages warm, personal, and brief — include sparkle emoji ✨
❌Never use cold, corporate language in any customer-facing communication
✅Test every mobile layout at 375px minimum before finalising
❌Never design desktop-first when 70%+ of Nigerian users are on mobile
